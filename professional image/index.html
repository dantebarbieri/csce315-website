Computer Science Contribution:

Computer Science is very important to me. I feel that it is important to work with others if great things are to be accomplished. It is with this thought in mind that I feel my greatest contribution to Computer Science is to help others understand the fundamental thought processes and logic that is encompassed by the field of Computer Science. I am a Peer Teacher for Texas A&M University's Computer Science team and I take great pride in that fact because people who learn to program are essentially studying the introductory parts of Computer Science and if I can instill in them even a single bit of my passion for Computer Science, I am sure that they would study this great field. I aspire to develop algorithms and to ponder data structures in the pursuit of solutions to challenging problems, however, at this point in time, it is unquestionable that my greatest contribution has been spreading it to as many people as I can come into contact with.

As happy as I am to be helping others by teaching them the fundamentals of Computer Science, I would be remiss if I did not also address how much the experiences help me learn. As notable physicist Richard Feynman codified in his Feynman Technique, the best way to truly learn something is to teach it. If I aim to actually develop performant algorithms and novel data structures, then it is best that I at least understand the fundamentals. It has been said that it is not good to reinvent the wheel, and this is true; but if you wish to build a better wheel, it is hard to find a better place to start than achieving complete mastery of the wheel that you seek to improve. With this in mind, I am delighted to be able to learn more about the things that I have learned in my classes by teaching the material to new students. Students as eager as I was and am to become the best Computer Scientists that we can.
